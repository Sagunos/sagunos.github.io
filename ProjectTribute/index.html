Miriam Defensor Santiago

"Dare beyond your strength, hazard beyond your judgement, and in extremeties, proceed in excellent hope. Bare the accidents of life with dignity and grace, making the best of circumstances."

Miriam Palma Defensor Santiago was a Filipina academic, lawyer, judge, author and stateswoman who served in all three branches of the Philippine government: judicial, executive and legislative.

  • 1945 - Born in Iloilo City
  • 1965 - Graduated Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science, Magna cum laude from the University of the Philippnes.
  • 1975 - Earned her Law degree from University of Michigan.
  • 1979 - Legal officer for United Nations High Commisioner for Refugees, Geneva,Switzerland.
  • 1988 - Appointed as Commissioner of the Bureau of Immigration and Deportation and directed raids against criminal syndicates, including Yakuza. She recieved death threats, but proclaimed: "I eat death threats for breakfast".
  • 1992 - Ran for Presidential election but was controversially defeated in events that involved a car crash injury and power outages during voting process.
  • 1995 - First elected Senator and was re-elected for another second and third term until 2016.She served for the foreign relations committee,exposed and named numerous jueteng(illegal gambling) lords throughout her term.
  • 2011 - Defensor Santiago was elected as judge of the International Criminal Court which hears cases against humanity for former heads of state.She was the first Asian from a third-world country to be elected to such post and resigned in 2014 after being diagnosed with lung cancer.
  • 2012 - Led the "Priority Development Assistance Fund scandal" which put the Senate President behind bars with charges of plunder for using Senate funds while the population lived in poverty.
  • 2015 - Announced her intention to run in the 2016 Philippine Presidential Election. Her campaign was focused on the youth sector for which she heavily advocated, making use of social media.She was a landslide winner in numerous polls.Despite this, she lost the election and was subsequentially called "The greated president we never had".
  • 2016 - First Filipina elected as a commissioner for the International Developement Law Organization Her role in the organization was advisory to the international law community. She died on September of the same year.

She wanted to take up literature in college, Her father asked if that's what she wanted but later changed her mind she said in an interview with the Philippine Star. "And we have a lot of relatives who are criminals, so you should be a lawyer. Many of my relatives were honor graduates but many of them were also cattle rustlers, or they were always murdering people or burning other people's houses.My father represented them all and acquitted most of them.That was the future laid out for me."
